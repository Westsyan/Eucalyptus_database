Introduction

Eucalyptus robusta,commonly known as swamp mahogany or swamp messmate,is a tree native to eastern Australia.Growing in swampy or waterlogged soils,it is up to 30 m (98 ft) high with thick spongy reddish brown bark and dark green broad leaves,which help from a dense canopy.The white to cream flowers appear in autumn and winter.The leaves are commonly eaten by insects,and are a food item for the koaia.It is an important winter-flowering species in eastern Australia,and has been planted extensively in many countries around the world.Its timber is used for firewood and in general construction.
